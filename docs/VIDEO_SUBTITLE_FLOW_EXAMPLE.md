# Video subtitle flow: why the flow fixer matters

The video is **animated manhua**—the same manhua with more frames and animation added to the pages. Each subtitle line corresponds to one on-screen moment (like a speech bubble or panel beat). The flow fixer should improve flow and consistency so that:

1. **Frame-by-frame:** Each line works as its own subtitle and fits its moment in the animation.
2. **In sequence:** When watched or read in order, the lines form a coherent narrative or monologue (no wrong pronouns, no choppy fragments that break the sense).

Checking the lines **as one paragraph** is a way to spot flow problems; the goal is not to merge lines into prose but to keep one subtitle per line while making the sequence work properly.

**As one paragraph (unchanged):**

> Registration Number: 1904073220604077. This seems to be the bus that took me to Chu Province for university. Aren't I undergoing the Heavenly Tribulation? Could it be that... I'm back. I Chen Beixuan. Around thirty years old. Taken away from Earth by Cangqin, the Immortal who travels the universe. Thus began the journey of cultivation to immortality. Because of his amazing talent. Reached the Tribulation stage in just 500 years. Known as the greatest in the cultivation world for tens of millions of years. the peerless genius with the greatest hope of successfully passing the Heavenly Tribulation. Unfortunately. My cultivation progress is too fast. unstable foundation. I couldn't withstand a single blow during the heart demon tribulation. I perished in the Heavenly Tribulation. These 500 years. For the sake of cultivation. I abandoned everything.

**Problems:**

- **Pronoun:** "Because of **his** amazing talent" — speaker is first person (I'm back, I, Chen Beixuan), so it should be **my**.
- **Choppy:** "I" then "Chen Beixuan" reads as "I Chen Beixuan" instead of "I'm Chen Beixuan" or a clear self-introduction.
- **Fragments:** "Unfortunately." "My cultivation progress is too fast." "unstable foundation." — could read more smoothly as connected thoughts.
- **Narrator shift:** "Thus began the journey" is third person; in first-person context it could be "Thus I began my journey."

The **flow fixer** (and translator) are instructed to fix these: first-person consistency (my not his), revising adjacent fragments when they are clearly one thought (e.g. "I" + "Chen Beixuan" → second line "I'm Chen Beixuan"), and smoother flow in sequence—while keeping one subtitle per line so it still works as animated manhua (one line per panel/beat).
